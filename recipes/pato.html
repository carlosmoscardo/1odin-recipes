Duck Breast Recipe

Description

Magret de pato is a dish made with the breast of a duck raised for foie gras, characterized by its juicy meat and a layer of fat that browns when cooked. It is prepared by scoring the skin in a diamond pattern and cooking it over medium heat, allowing the fat to melt and form a crispy crust while the inside remains pink and tender.

Once cooked, the magret is sliced thinly and is often served with sweet-and-sour sauces, such as red wine reduction, red fruits, or orange. It is a refined and tasty dish, where the balance between juicy meat and crispy skin makes it an exquisite option to enjoy an elegant and flavorful preparation.

Ingredients

  • Magret de pato
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Red wine
  • Honey
  • Red fruits
  • Orange
  • Balsamic vinegar
  • Butter
  • Rosemary

Steps to follow

  1. Score the skin of the magret in a diamond pattern without cutting the meat, to help the fat melt.
  2. Season both sides of the magret with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Heat a pan without oil over medium heat and place the magret skin-side down, allowing the fat to melt and the skin to become crispy.
  4. Cook for 6-8 minutes, then flip it over and sear the other side for another 3-4 minutes, until the inside is pink.
  5. Remove the magret and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it thinly.
  6. Prepare the sauce by reducing red wine, honey, red fruits, and balsamic vinegar over medium heat until you get a thick texture.
  7. Plate the dish, serving the sliced magret with the sauce on top or on the side.
